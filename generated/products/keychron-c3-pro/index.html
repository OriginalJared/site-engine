Our Review

The Keychron C3 Pro rewrites what's possible at $37. It has features that cost $100+ just two years ago: a gasket-mounted design for a softer typing feel, hot-swappable switches, QMK/VIA firmware for full key remapping, and red backlight LEDs. The pre-lubed Keychron mechanical switches feel smooth out of the box, and the double-shot ABS keycaps are durable enough for daily use. The wired USB-C connection means zero latency and no battery to charge. PCMag gave it an Editors' Choice as the best budget mechanical keyboard. If you've never tried a mechanical keyboard, this is where to start.
