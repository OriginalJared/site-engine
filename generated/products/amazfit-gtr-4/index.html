Our Review

The Amazfit GTR 4 delivers flagship-level hardware at half the price of Apple and Samsung. The 1.43-inch AMOLED display is bright and responsive, battery life reaches 14 days, and the dual-band GPS is accurate for outdoor activities. Health tracking covers heart rate, blood oxygen, stress, and sleep with Amazfit's Zepp OS providing a clean interface. It works with both Android and iPhone — no ecosystem lock-in. The stainless steel case and leather/silicone band options make it look more expensive than it is. At $200, it's the best value smartwatch for people who want health tracking without the Apple/Samsung tax.
